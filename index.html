Common questions

Answers,
honestly.

Have a question we haven't covered? Write us — we read everything.

Is Guilder actually free for homeowners?+
Yes. We earn a 6–8% referral fee from the contractor when a project closes. Homeowners pay nothing for the analysis, incentive discovery, roadmap, or contractor matching. Contractors pay it because pre-scoped leads convert far better than cold prospects.
How is this different from a free online calculator?+
Most calculators use lookup tables — "a 2,000 sq ft home in Connecticut averages X in heating costs." We actually simulate your house: heat flow equations, your real R-values, the specific degree days for your town, roof orientation and pitch, and the age and efficiency of your equipment. You get a number that's specific to your home, not an average of homes near it.
How accurate is an analysis without a site visit?+
Satellite imagery gives us roof geometry, orientation, and shading. Assessor records give us year built, square footage, construction type. Your equipment photos tell us exact age and efficiency. Combined with 12 months of utility data, this produces projections comparable to a preliminary walkthrough. Contractors refine the final scope — our job is to get you there with confidence and a clear plan.
What data do I need to give you?+
Three things: your address, a utility bill (PDF or photo — we pull 12 months from it), and a photo of your major equipment (water heater, furnace, or the label on the back of the unit). That's it. We never ask for your Social Security number, bank details, or credit info.
What if the IRA or state incentives change?+
Our engine tracks regulatory changes daily and updates eligibility in real time. State and utility programs historically have filled gaps when federal programs change. The process is complex enough that if anything, regulatory change makes us more valuable — not less.
Do you handle any kind of renovation, or just energy projects?+
Only energy and electrification projects. Heat pumps, solar, insulation, batteries, EV chargers, induction. We don't scope kitchens or bathrooms — that's not where the climate and cost impact is. Every project we match is energy-impact positive.
When will Guilder be available in my area?+
We're live now in Fairfield County, CT, and expanding across Connecticut through 2027, then into Westchester County, NY and greater New England. Drop your address below and we'll let you know the moment your county is covered.
